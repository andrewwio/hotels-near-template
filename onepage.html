Best Things to Do Near Hard Rock Tampa

Looking for some of the best things to do in Tampa? The city's attractions are much more exciting than just Hard Rock. Let's check out some of the top places to visit on your trip to fill in time at the Hard Rock waiting for your event. We've curated a list of the top places to visit near Hard Rock.

A quick reminder about transportation: renting a car or calling Uber is your best option. Also, don't forget your sunblock and don't forget your walking shoes!

Manatee Viewing Center

One of the local favorites is the Manatee Viewing Center. Stationed just outside the Tampa Electric Company, the power plant safely discharged warm ocean water into Tampa Bay, where nearby marine life gathers to enjoy a break from the cooler temperatures of the bay. Here, you can see manatees, dolphins, sharks, and tarpons. They tend to be more active in the morning, when the water is cooler. If you weren't able to see the manatees, you can check out the stingray petting tank where you can even touch the stingrays! Don't worry, their barbs have been removed so they are completely safe to pet. There's also a nature trail that leads to a 50ft observation tower for some nice views of the bay. The park is open from 10am-5pm every day. And the best part is it's totally free to visit!

Lettuce Lake Nature Park

Lettuce Lake is one of the best nature parks Tampa has to offer. It's located on the Hillsborough river and it's a glimpse of what Florida was like before civilization. Not only is it a great place to walk and bike for some fresh air, but there's a good chance you'll get to see some wild animals too. Spotting alligators, turtles, armadillos, and spoonbills are common here! There's tons of fun activities like renting kayaks, enjoying a picnic under the pavilions, or just strolling along the well-maintained boardwalks. The park is open from 8am-6pm every day and the entry fee is $2.

Tampa Riverwalk

Next on this list is none other than the Tampa Riverwalk. This 2.5mi stretch runs along the Hillsborough river through the heart of downtown where you'll not only find some of the best restaurants, outdoor events, and nightlife, but also some of the city's best attractions like the Florida Aquarium, the American Victory Ship & Museum, and the Tampa Bay History Center. The Riverwalk can be enjoyed during the day or in the evening, and it's great fun for kids and adults alike. Paid parking garages and street parking can be found throughout the area.

Busch Gardens

We'd be remiss not to mention the main attraction of Tampa entertainment: Busch Gardens. Busch Gardens is an Africa-themed theme park, home to a dozen rides and over 2,000 animals. For the brave, try out SheiKra, a coaster with a 200-ft drop followed by a series of loops, turns, and even goes underground! Once you've had all the rollercoasters you can handle, take the Serengeti Safari where you can feed giraffes, enjoy an ice skating show at the Moroccan Palace Theater, or cool off on the Congo Rapid water ride. Busch Gardens will definitely be a memorable adventure for all. One thing to note, if you happen to visit between June and October, expect there to be rain in the afternoon. It usually starts are 2pm or 3pm and goes for a couple hours leaving another couple hours to enjoy the sunset. The park opens at 10am but the rides begin operation at 11am. The park closes at 10pm, tickets start at $89 + $30 for parking.
